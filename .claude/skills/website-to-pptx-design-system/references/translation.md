# Translation catalog: web → slide

The detail behind Output **Section 7 (Component Rules)** and **Section 10 (Recommended Slide Templates)**. Adapt these to the brand you measured — they are the vocabulary, not a fixed script. Keep the final set **constrained**: pick the components and templates the deck actually needs.

A recurring rule: **a website button is not a slide button.** There is nothing to click on a slide. Everywhere a component below descends from a web button, it becomes a *callout, tag, label, or emphasis device* — never a fake clickable control, never a lone pill floating mid-slide.

---

## Part A — Component rules (Section 7)

For each: **structure · visual treatment · best use · mistake to avoid.**

### Title block
- **Structure:** kicker/eyebrow (optional) + headline + optional deck sub-line.
- **Treatment:** headline in the brand display font at title size; kicker in the accent or a muted caption; align left unless the brand is emphatically centered.
- **Best use:** cover, section dividers, any slide that opens a new idea.
- **Avoid:** a thin accent rule under the title — the #1 AI-slide tell. Use size, weight, and whitespace for separation instead.

### Section divider
- **Structure:** big section number or label + section title on a distinct background.
- **Treatment:** put dividers on the brand's *other* base surface so they punctuate the deck rhythmically — a **light-native** brand flips to its dark surface (the footer/CTA-band color); a **dark-native** brand has no lighter ground to borrow, so flip to a distinct panel (one step off the ground) or a deeper black carrying the accent. Display font, large.
- **Best use:** between major parts of the argument.
- **Avoid:** dividers that look identical to content slides — they should feel like a breath.

### Callout box (from web buttons / highlight panels)
- **Structure:** short label or one-line statement inside a tinted container.
- **Treatment:** muted brand tint background (~8–12% of brand primary) with brand-primary or ink text and the brand's corner radius; **no border stripe**.
- **Best use:** a takeaway, a definition, a "so what," the translated CTA.
- **Avoid:** styling it as a glossy 3D button; overusing it so every slide has one.

### Metric card (stat callout)
- **Structure:** big number (the hero) + short label + optional delta.
- **Treatment:** number at 60–72pt in the display font, brand primary or ink; label small and muted beneath. Row of 2–4 on a surface.
- **Best use:** traction, results, "by the numbers" slides.
- **Avoid:** burying the number in body-size type; more than 4 across (they stop being scannable).

### Quote card
- **Structure:** quotation + attribution (name, role, logo).
- **Treatment:** quote in display font, generous leading; attribution in muted caption. A large soft quote mark in the accent is a good on-brand motif if the site has that personality.
- **Best use:** testimonials, expert voice, customer proof.
- **Avoid:** centering a long quote (hard to read) or setting it in tiny type to fit.

### Image frame
- **Structure:** photo/illustration in a consistent container.
- **Treatment:** apply the brand's corner radius and shadow language uniformly. Pick **one** frame style and repeat it across the deck — a strong, cheap visual motif.
- **Best use:** any slide that needs a human or contextual image.
- **Avoid:** mixing rounded and sharp frames, or drop shadows of different depths, across slides.

### Product screenshot frame
- **Structure:** the app UI inside a neutral device/browser frame, or cropped cleanly to a key region.
- **Treatment:** put the screenshot on a muted brand surface (not pure white on white) so it reads as an object; add a subtle shadow. **Crop to the point** — one feature per slide, zoomed enough to read.
- **Best use:** product/feature slides, demos, before/after.
- **Avoid:** shrinking a full-page capture until the UI text is illegible; busy backgrounds that fight the UI.

### Table
- **Structure:** header row + body rows, minimal rules.
- **Treatment:** no heavy gridlines — use a tinted header row and generous row height. Right-align numbers; align the highlighted column with an accent tint.
- **Best use:** comparisons, plans, specs.
- **Avoid:** dense spreadsheet grids; more columns than a viewer can scan in a few seconds.

### Timeline
- **Structure:** ordered points along a horizontal or vertical spine.
- **Treatment:** node markers in the brand palette, labels in caption size; keep to 3–5 stops.
- **Best use:** history, sequence, milestones.
- **Avoid:** cramming a year-by-year history — collapse to the meaningful phases.

### Process diagram
- **Structure:** numbered steps connected by arrows or chevrons.
- **Treatment:** each step a small card or numbered token in the brand palette; consistent sizing; 3–5 steps.
- **Best use:** how-it-works, methodology.
- **Avoid:** more than ~6 steps on one slide; inconsistent arrow styles.

### Comparison (two-column)
- **Structure:** two (or three) parallel columns — before/after, us/them, options.
- **Treatment:** columns equal width; use the accent to mark the favored side, a muted surface for the other. Align rows so items compare left-to-right.
- **Best use:** positioning, tradeoffs, migration value.
- **Avoid:** stacking the two sides vertically (kills the comparison); coloring the "bad" side in an alarming red unless the brand actually uses it.

### Problem / solution
- **Structure:** the tension on one side or slide, the resolution on the next.
- **Treatment:** problem on the darker/muted surface, solution on the bright brand surface — let color carry the turn.
- **Best use:** narrative pivot in a pitch.
- **Avoid:** listing problem and solution as two equal bullet lists with no visual shift.

### CTA / closing element
- **Structure:** one clear ask + the essential contact/next-step info.
- **Treatment:** brand dark surface, display headline, the accent used once. Contact details in a clean caption row — **not** a fake button.
- **Best use:** the final slide.
- **Avoid:** a slide-wide colored bar or a mock "Sign up" button; more than one ask.

## Part B — Slide template catalog (Section 10)

The practical set. For each: **purpose · layout · visual rules · when to use.** Not every deck needs all of them — recommend the subset that fits the user's purpose, and note it.

### Cover
- **Purpose:** set brand and subject in one glance.
- **Layout:** display headline + sub-line, logo, date/author in a caption; often on the brand dark surface or a full-bleed brand image.
- **Rules:** biggest type in the deck; accent used once; motif introduced here.
- **When:** slide 1, always.

### Agenda
- **Purpose:** map the argument.
- **Layout:** numbered list or a 2-column set of labeled items with short descriptions.
- **Rules:** parallel phrasing; numbers in the display font; no more than ~5 items.
- **When:** decks over ~8 slides or with a formal audience.

### Section divider
- **Purpose:** signal a new part.
- **Layout:** large section number + title on the brand dark surface.
- **Rules:** visually distinct from content slides; consistent across all dividers.
- **When:** between major sections.

### Executive summary
- **Purpose:** the whole story on one slide for people who won't hear the rest.
- **Layout:** 3–4 stacked takeaways, or a metric row + a one-line thesis.
- **Rules:** lead with conclusions, not setup; each point stands alone.
- **When:** early in exec/board decks.

### Problem framing
- **Purpose:** establish the tension worth solving.
- **Layout:** a stark statement + supporting evidence (stat or image); muted/dark surface.
- **Rules:** one problem, stated plainly; resist bullet soup.
- **When:** after the cover in a pitch.

### Insight
- **Purpose:** the non-obvious "aha" that reframes the problem.
- **Layout:** one large sentence, lots of whitespace, maybe one supporting mark.
- **Rules:** minimal — the idea carries the slide; accent on the key phrase.
- **When:** the pivot from problem to solution.

### Product screenshot
- **Purpose:** show the thing working.
- **Layout:** framed screenshot on a muted surface + a short caption or 2–3 annotations.
- **Rules:** crop to one feature; readable UI text; consistent frame (see component).
- **When:** product, demo, feature deep-dives.

### Metrics
- **Purpose:** prove traction/results.
- **Layout:** 2–4 metric cards in a row, optional supporting chart.
- **Rules:** big numbers; one insight per number; source in a caption.
- **When:** traction, results, QBR slides.

### Process
- **Purpose:** explain how something works or gets done.
- **Layout:** 3–5 step process diagram.
- **Rules:** consistent step tokens; a single clause per step.
- **When:** methodology, onboarding, how-it-works.

### Comparison
- **Purpose:** position against alternatives or show before/after.
- **Layout:** two/three aligned columns (see component).
- **Rules:** accent marks the favored side; rows align for scanning.
- **When:** competitive, migration, options.

### Roadmap
- **Purpose:** show what's coming and when.
- **Layout:** horizontal timeline or now/next/later columns.
- **Rules:** phases over exact dates unless dates matter; 3–4 buckets.
- **When:** planning, vision, investor updates.

### Recommendation
- **Purpose:** state the decision you want.
- **Layout:** the recommendation as a headline + a short rationale list or a callout.
- **Rules:** unambiguous; the ask is the biggest thing on the slide.
- **When:** decision and strategy decks.

### Closing
- **Purpose:** land the ask and how to follow up.
- **Layout:** the CTA/closing element — one ask + contact caption on the brand dark surface.
- **Rules:** mirror the cover for bookend symmetry; accent once.
- **When:** final slide, always.
